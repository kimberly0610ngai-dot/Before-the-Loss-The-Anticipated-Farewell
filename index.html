在失去之前：預期的告別 Before the Loss: The Anticipated Farewell

王藝寰 Wong Ngai Wan

Wong Ngai Wan

「哀傷是無聲的綵排，在心裡一遍遍上演。」 "Grief is a silent rehearsal—played again and again in the heart."

展覽理念Exhibition Concept

每當祖母再次因為病情反覆進出醫院，我總在日常的細碎瞬間中，提前預演失去的畫面。

哀傷是無聲的綵排，在心裡一遍遍上演，讓我提早哭泣和道別，也提早學會珍惜現在的每一刻。

本系列作品《Before the Loss: The Anticipated Farewell》以「預期性悲傷」為核心，通過五個場景，具象化我在面對祖母反覆進出醫院的歲月中，內心所經歷的五個非線性情緒階段。

展覽最後設有「四道人生」留言區。願我的這段經歷，能成為一個開端，邀請你一起思考：如何更溫柔地對待我們所愛的人，如何在有限的時光裡，及時表達愛。

Whenever my grandmother was admitted to hospital once more due to recurring illness, I would rehearse scenes of loss in the minutiae of daily life.

Grief is a silent rehearsal, played over and over in the mind, allowing me to weep and bid farewell prematurely, yet also to learn to cherish each present moment.

This series, "Before the Loss: The Anticipated Farewell", centres on the concept of anticipatory grief. Through five distinct scenes, it visualises the five non-linear emotional stages I experienced internally during the years of witnessing my grandmother's repeated hospitalisations.

The exhibition concludes with a "The Four Paths of Life" message wall. May my experience serve as a starting point, inviting you to reflect: how might we treat our loved ones with greater tenderness? How can we express our affection in the limited time we have?

理論背景：預期性悲傷 Theoretical Framework: Five Stages of Anticipatory Grief

Elisabeth Kübler-Ross (1969) 提出了「悲傷五階段」模型，用以解釋人們面對絕症與重大喪失時的情緒與心理歷程。這五個階段分別為否認、憤怒、討價還價、抑鬱與接受。這些階段並非必須依序完成的線性固定流程。相反地，它們代表著心理調適的流動動態過程，個體可能在不同階段間交替出現（Kübler-Ross & Kessler, 2005）。當此過程發生於實際喪失之前——例如得知至親罹患絕症且預後不良時——即稱為「預期性悲傷」。

有別於傳統哀悼，預期性悲傷發生於實際喪失尚未發生時，當事人需同時處理兩種現實：迫在眉睫的喪失與當下擁有的事物（Rando, 1986）。

Elisabeth Kübler-Ross (1969) proposed the Five Stages of Grief model to explain the emotional and psychological process when people face terminal illness and significant loss. The five stages are denial, anger, bargaining, depression, and acceptance. They are not a linear, fixed sequence that must be completed. On the contrary, they represent a fluid, dynamic process of psychological adjustment, where individuals may alternate between different stages (Kübler-Ross & Kessler, 2005). When this process occurs before the actual loss such as following a poor prognosis for a loved one's terminal illness, it is termed "anticipatory grief".

Different from traditional mourning, anticipatory grief occurs before the actual loss materializes, with the individual simultaneously processing two realities: the impending loss and the present possession (Rando, 1986).

1. 否認 Denial 1. Denial

否認作為心理緩衝機制，能讓個體以可承受的速度逐步接受迫近喪失的震撼現實（Kübler-Ross, 1969）。其特徵包含難以置信、情緒麻木，以及有意識或無意識地拒絕接受預後結果。在預期性悲傷情境中，否認表現為緊抓正常生活不放，並迴避疾病相關話題（Rando, 1986）。此階段未必是脆弱的徵兆，而是暫時避風港，用以抵禦洶湧的情感風暴。

Denial, as a psychological buffer, serves as a defense mechanism that allows individuals to accept the shocking reality of impending loss at a manageable pace (Kübler-Ross, 1969). It is characterized by disbelief, numbness, and a conscious or unconscious refusal to accept the prognosis. In the context of anticipatory grief, denial manifests as clinging to normalcy and avoiding conversations about the illness (Rando, 1986). This stage is not necessarily a sign of weakness but a temporary refuge to shield oneself from overwhelming emotional turmoil.

2. 憤怒 Anger 2. Anger

庫伯勒-羅斯（1969）指出，當否認的保護層消散，痛苦重新浮現並轉化為憤怒時，憤怒便可能湧現（Kübler-Ross, 1969）。這種強烈情緒可能充滿非理性且指向錯誤對象，如家人、醫護人員或更高權力。此憤怒的核心往往源於深刻的不公感與無力感（Kübler-Ross & Kessler, 2005）。在預期性悲傷中，個體同時經歷憤怒情緒與不可改變的現實。

Kübler-Ross (1969) pointed out that the emergence of anger may occur as the protection of denial dissipates, allowing pain to resurface and transform into anger. This intense emotion can be irrational and misdirected, targeted at family members, medical staff, or a higher power. The core of this anger is often a profound sense of unfairness and powerlessness (Kübler-Ross & Kessler, 2005). In anticipatory grief, the individual simultaneously experiences the emotion of anger and the unalterable reality.

3. 討價還價 Bargaining 3. Bargaining

庫伯勒-羅絲（1969）指出，個體在討價還價階段會試圖透過與更高權力的協商，重新掌控局面並延緩不可避免的結局。此階段可解讀為當事人試圖尋找機會，阻止或延緩所愛之人離去的結局。在預期性悲傷中，討價還價充滿「要是⋯⋯該多好」的假設性陳述與暗中交易，反映出對不可控事物產生影響的絕望期盼（Rando, 1986）。

Kübler-Ross (1969) pointed out that the individual seeks to regain control and postpone the inevitable through negotiation with a higher power in the bargaining stage. It can be explained as an individual's attempt to find opportunities to prevent or postpone the result of the departure of a loved one. In anticipatory grief, bargaining is filled with "if only" statements and secret deals, reflecting a desperate hope to influence the uncontrollable (Rando, 1986).

4. 抑鬱 Depression 4. Depression

此階段標誌著直面喪失之痛的開端。抑鬱是憤怒與討價還價情緒的消退，取而代之的是對既成與即將到來之損失的深切悲慟與哀悼（Kübler-Ross, 1969）。此非病態抑鬱，而是適宜的預備性哀傷（Kübler-Ross & Kessler, 2005）。此階段伴隨自我封閉、靜默沉思，以及對即將到來最終分離的壓倒性悲慟。

This stage marks the beginning of confronting the raw and profound reality of the loss. Depression is a subsidence of anger and bargaining, replaced by deep sadness and mourning for the losses that are already occurring and the ones that are to come (Kübler-Ross, 1969). This is not a pathological depression but an appropriate, preparatory grief (Kübler-Ross & Kessler, 2005). It involves withdrawal, quiet reflection, and overwhelming sorrow for the impending final separation.

5. 接受 Acceptance 5. Acceptance

接受並非指欣然接受或對喪失的屈服，而是與現實達成和解（Kübler-Ross, 1969）。此階段被詮釋為情感抽離與靜默準備。在預期性悲傷中，這是珍貴的時機，讓人得以與臨終者進行深具意義的對話，並以靜默而充滿愛的存在陪伴（Rando, 2000）。接納的特徵在於經歷前期情感掙扎後，內心湧現平和安寧之感。

Acceptance is not defined as happiness or resignation to the loss, but rather about coming to terms with its reality (Kübler-Ross, 1969). It is a stage explained as emotional detachment and quiet preparation. In anticipatory grief, it is a precious period that allows for meaningful conversations and a quiet, loving presence with the dying person (Rando, 2000). Acceptance is characterized by an emotion of peace and calm after the struggle of preceding emotions.

四道人生 The Four Paths of Life

善別，始於善生。 A good farewell begins with living well.

何謂「四道人生」？

「四道人生」是源自華人安寧照護的智慧框架，鼓勵我們在時間仍充裕時，主動且完整地經營重要關係。這四句話——道愛、道謝、道歉、道別——旨在讓生命終了時，只留愛，不留遺憾。

這是一套為生命關係「完成」與「和解」而設的實踐路徑。它並非僅用於臨終時刻，而是適用於任何珍視的關係中，透過真誠的表達，讓愛流動，讓傷痛療癒，讓告別帶著平靜與祝福。

感謝您在此停留，現在邀請您停下腳步，在留言板寫下您的感悟、感謝、或一句想說的話。

What are the "Four Paths of Life"?

The "Four Paths of Life" is a wisdom framework from Chinese palliative care, which encourages us to proactively and fully nurture our important relationships while time still allows. These four expressions—Expressing Love, Gratitude, Forgiveness/Apology, and Farewell—aim to ensure that when life concludes, only love remains, not regret.

This is a set of practical paths for the "completion" and "reconciliation" of life's relationships. It is not only for life's final moments but is applicable to any cherished relationship. Through sincere expression, it allows love to flow, wounds to heal, and farewells to be made with peace and blessing.

道愛 Expressing Love

愛，需要被聽見。「道愛」是將心底的情感，化為清晰、真摯的言語或行動，明確地傳遞給所愛之人。它讓溫暖得以確證，讓關係充滿光的溫度。

"Love is the attempt to understand."

「愛是努力去理解。」

——村上春樹《挪威的森林》

"Love begins at home, and it is not how much we do... but how much love we put in that action."

——德蕾莎修女

Love needs to be heard. "Expressing Love" is the clear and sincere translation of inner affection into words or actions, delivered explicitly to those we love. It allows warmth to be confirmed and fills relationships with the temperature of light.

"Love is the attempt to understand." — Haruki Murakami

"Love begins at home, and it is not how much we do... but how much love we put in that action." — Mother Teresa

道謝 Expressing Gratitude

謝，值得被銘記。「道謝」是對生命中所獲得的幫助、陪伴與祝福，誠心地道一聲感謝。它梳理記憶的豐盛，讓施與受的善意，成為彼此生命的養分。

「願君多採擷，此物最相思。」

——王維《相思》

「讓我們永遠不要因忙碌而忘了表達謝意。」

——德蕾莎修女

「滴水之恩，當湧泉相報。」

——中國諺語

Gratitude deserves to be remembered. "Expressing Gratitude" is the heartfelt act of thanking others for the help, companionship, and blessings received in life. It sorts through the abundance of memory, allowing the goodwill of giving and receiving to become nourishment for each other's lives.

"May you gather more of this, for it holds the deepest memory of love." — Wang Wei

"Never let a day pass without saying the words 'I love you' and 'thank you' to those you love." — Mother Teresa

"For a drop of kindness, return a spring of gratitude." — Chinese Proverb

道歉 Expressing Forgiveness/Apology

恕，為了得自由。「道歉」包含歉意與寬恕。是放下驕傲，尋求和解；也是釋懷傷痛，解開枷鎖。它修復裂痕，讓沉重的心事得以輕裝前行。

"Forgiveness says you are given another chance to make a new beginning."

——戴斯蒙·屠圖

"Forgiveness is not an occasional act; it is a constant attitude."

——馬丁·路德·金

「躬自厚而薄責於人，則遠怨矣。」

——孔子

Forgiveness, for the sake of freedom. "Expressing Forgiveness/Apology" encompasses both offering apology and granting forgiveness. It is about letting go of pride to seek reconciliation and releasing pain to break chains. It mends fractures, allowing burdensome matters of the heart to move forward lightly.

"Forgiveness says you are given another chance to make a new beginning." — Desmond Tutu

"Forgiveness is not an occasional act; it is a constant attitude." — Martin Luther King Jr.

"Demand much from yourself but little from others, and you will keep resentment at bay." — Confucius

道別 Saying Goodbye

別，為了好好續。「道別」不是消極的永訣，而是積極的完成。是珍重祝福，是託付思念，是將彼此的故事安放於心，然後帶著愛，繼續各自的旅程。

「死別之痛，證明了愛曾真實存在。」

——C.S.路易斯《A Grief Observed》

「人生天地間，忽如遠行客。」

——《古詩十九首》

Farewell, for a good continuation. "Saying Farewell" is not a passive ending but an active completion. It is an exchange of heartfelt blessings, the entrusting of memories, the safekeeping of each other's stories in the heart, and then continuing on our respective journeys, carrying love forward.

"The pain I feel now is the happiness I had before. That's the deal." — C.S. Lewis, A Grief Observed

"We live between heaven and earth, suddenly like travelers on a long journey." — Nineteen Old Poems

關於藝術家 About the Artist

藝術家簡介 Artist Bio

王藝寰 (Kimberli) 是香港教育大學藝術系學生，同時攻讀創意藝術與文化榮譽文學士及視覺藝術榮譽教育學士雙學位。求學期間，她持續探索多元藝術媒介，專精於混合媒介創作。其創作聚焦香港本土文化，致力喚起社會對城市重要議題的關注。

透過本地學校及藝術機構的實習經歷，Kimberli 期望運用藝術揭示香港未被看見的社會故事與議題。畢業後，她計劃結合教學經驗與創新藝術表達，為香港藝術界貢獻心力。

KIMBERLI Wong Ngai Wan is an art student currently pursuing a double degree BA (Honours) in Creative Arts and Culture and a BEd (Honours) in Visual Arts at The Education University of Hong Kong. Throughout her studies, Kimberli has experimented with various art mediums and specializes in mixed-media creations. Her work focuses on Hong Kong's local culture and aims to draw attention to important social issues in the city.

Through internships at secondary schools and art organizations, Kimberli seeks to use her art to highlight unseen social stories and concerns in Hong Kong. After graduation, she aims to contribute to Hong Kong's art scene by combining her teaching experience with innovative artistic expression.

藝術家自述 Artist Statement

我的創作探索人類情感中抽象的失落過程。

這件名為《準備失去》的裝置藝術，啟發自庫伯勒-羅斯模型，探究我們面對失去時所經歷的複雜心理歷程。

透過一系列藝術品，我將理論中的五個階段——否認、憤怒、討價還價、抑鬱與接受——轉化為靜謐而共鳴的場景。運用日常熟悉的物件，我試圖訴說那些未被言說的感受。這些並非誇張的哀悼姿態，而是心靈過渡期中靜默而執著的印記。

裝置主張悲傷並非待治癒的缺陷，而是人類深層的保存機制、協商過程，最終通往靜謐而艱辛的平和。這是一場視覺冥想，探討我們如何緊握、如何放手，以及如何在兩者間的空隙中尋得意義。

My work explores the idea of human emotion of the abstract processes of loss.

This installation, titled "Preparation of loss" draws upon the Kübler-Ross model to investigate the complex psychological landscape we traverse when facing an impending, profound loss.

Through a series of installations, I transform the theoretical stages of Denial, Anger, Bargaining, Depression, and Acceptance into quiet, resonant scenes. By using familiar, everyday objects, I seek to articulate the unspoken. These are not grand gestures of mourning, but the quiet, stubborn evidence of a heart in transition.

The installation posits that grief is not a flaw to be cured, but a deeply human process of preservation, negotiation, and, ultimately, a quiet, hard-won peace. It is a visual meditation on how we hold on, how we let go, and how we find meaning in the space between.
